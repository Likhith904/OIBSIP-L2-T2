S. S. Rajamouli

Koduri Srisaila Sri Rajamouli, better known as S. S. Rajamouli, is an Indian film director and screenwriter. He is known for directing blockbuster films like "RRR", "Baahubali: The Beginning", "Baahubali: The Conclusion", and "Magadheera."

Early life and career

  • 1) He was born on 10 October 1973 in Amareshwara Camp in Raichur district (present-day Karnataka state) to K. V. Vijayendra Prasad and Raja Nandini. His father is a screenwriter and his mother was a housewife.
  • 2) He spent his childhood moving between Kovvur and Eluru in Andhra Pradesh due to his father's job. He completed his engineering degree at C.R. Reddy College of Engineering in Eluru.
  • 3) He made his debut as a director with Student No.1 in 2001, which was a commercial success. His second film, Simhadri, was released in 2003 and was also a hit.
  • 4) He gained fame and recognition with his third film, Sye, which was based on a rugby game and was the first of its kind in Tollywood. His next film, Chatrapati, was also a blockbuster.
  • 5) He achieved national and international acclaim with his 2009 film, Magadheera, which was a fantasy action film and the highest-grossing Telugu film ever at the time of its release . It was also remade in several languages, such as Hindi, Tamil, and Malayalam.
  • 6) He continued his success streak with films like Maryada Ramanna, Eega, Baahubali: The Beginning, Baahubali 2: The Conclusion, and RRR. All of these films were critically and commercially acclaimed and broke several records at the box office.
  • 7) He has won four National Film Awards, two Saturn Awards, a New York Film Critics Circle award, a Critics' Choice Movie Award, and many other honours for his films. He was also awarded the Padma Shri by the Government of India in 2016 for his contributions to the field of art.
  • 8) He is regarded as one of the most influential and innovative filmmakers in India and has been praised by many celebrities and critics for his vision and creativity . He is also credited for expanding the market of South Indian cinema and international markets .

Baahubali Series

  • 1) Director and Visionary: S. S. Rajamouli, a renowned Indian film director and screenwriter, directed the "Baahubali" series. His vision and storytelling prowess were instrumental in the success of the films.
  • 2) Epic Fantasy Saga: "Baahubali" is an epic fantasy saga that consists of two films: "Baahubali: The Beginning" (2015) and "Baahubali 2: The Conclusion" (2017). The series is known for its grand scale, visual effects, and gripping narrative.
  • 3) Record-breaking Box Office: The "Baahubali" series shattered box office records in India and globally. "Baahubali 2: The Conclusion" became the highest-grossing Indian film at the time of its release, garnering immense commercial success.
  • 4) Intricate Plot and Characters: The series follows the story of Amarendra Baahubali and Bhallaladeva, two cousins vying for the throne of Mahishmati. The plot is woven with intricate characters, political intrigue, and intense emotions.
  • 5) Impressive Cast: The series features a stellar cast, including Prabhas as Baahubali, Rana Daggubati as Bhallaladeva, Anushka Shetty as Devasena, Tamannaah as Avanthika, and Ramya Krishnan as Sivagami, among others. Their performances contributed to the film's success.
  • 6) Technological Marvel: The "Baahubali" series showcased cutting-edge VFX and special effects, raising the bar for Indian cinema. The grand sets, battle sequences, and visual grandeur were a significant highlight.
  • 7) International Recognition: The films received international acclaim and were screened at various film festivals, introducing Indian cinema to a global audience. The series won several awards for its technical excellence.
  • 8) Cult Following: "Baahubali" developed a massive cult following, with fans eagerly anticipating each installment. The dialogues, music, and action sequences became iconic, contributing to the film's popularity.
  • 9) Multi-lingual Release: The films were released in multiple languages, including Telugu, Tamil, Hindi, Malayalam, and others, to cater to a broader audience, further boosting their reach and impact.
  • 10) Prequel and Sequel: "Baahubali: The Beginning" set the stage for the story, while "Baahubali 2: The Conclusion" concluded the epic tale. Together, they provided a comprehensive and satisfying narrative experience.

Other Works

  • 1) Magadheera (2009): A historical fantasy film that received critical acclaim and became a major box office success, establishing Rajamouli as a prominent director in the Indian film industry.
  • 2) Eega (2012): A fantasy film that garnered critical acclaim for its unique storyline and visual effects. The film won several awards and was praised for its innovative storytelling.
  • 3) Sye (2004): A sports action film based on rugby, showcasing Rajamouli's versatility in exploring different genres within Indian cinema.
  • 4) Maryada Ramanna (2010): A comedy-drama film known for its entertaining storyline and engaging performances, demonstrating Rajamouli's skill in various genres.
  • 5) Chatrapati (2005): An action drama film featuring Prabhas in the lead role. The film received critical acclaim for its compelling storyline and Prabhas's performance, further establishing him as a promising actor.
  • 6) RRR (2022): A fearless revolutionary and an officer in the British force, who once shared a deep bond, decide to join forces and chart out an inspirational path of freedom against the despotic rulers. Interesting fact is that a song in this film called "Naatu Naatu" won oscars in Best Original Song category in 95th Academy Awards.

Personal Life

  • 1) He is married to Rama Rajamouli, a costume designer who has worked on most of his films . The couple has two children, a daughter named S.S. Mayookha and a son named S.S. Karthikeya
  • 2) He comes from a family deeply associated with the Telugu film industry.
  • 3) He was also listed among the world's 100 most influential people of 2023 by Time magazine .
  • 4) Rajamouli has won several awards for his work, including the Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song for the track “Naatu Naatu" from the Telugu film RRR (2022) at the 80th Golden Globes .
  • 5) In January 2023, his film RRR won two awards at the 28th annual Critics Choice Awards at The Fairmont Century Plaza Hotel in Los Angeles .
